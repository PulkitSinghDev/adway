You’re all chasing the same keywords and town names. So there will be a dozen roofing companies looking to get that person’s click. Google will only show the first so many ads – whoever pays the most, basically. And roofing companies are paying a stupid amount of money – $80 and more at times – for each click. Many of which end without a sale for them. Google ads are very useful for niche businesses operating in unusual or lower-competition industries. But in competitive industries like residential roofing? Google ads is a lottery for the roofer because he’s paying top dollar per click for a large number of people who are ‘just looking’. You can burn through a thousand dollars a day just on the ‘looking around’ crowd. And when a serious buyer sees your ad… he’s also seeing 4 or 5 of your competitors’ ads at the same time. So if he buys, he calls all 5 of you first and gets the lowest rate he can. So you’re paying $80 and more per click, most clicks lead to no sale and, when you do get a sale…. the value has been ripped out of it because you had to out-price 5 other companies. Facebook ads, on the other hand, aren’t responding to a person’s search. They’re presenting themselves to a person because their profile and Facebook usage habits make them a good candidate for being a roofing customer. If he’s not looking for a new roof he doesn’t click. So no wasted dollars there. If he really is in the market for a new roof he clicks, he books his roof inspection with you… and you just got yourself an already half-done roofing deal. He saw your ad only. So that lead is yours, exclusively. There’s no other roofing company – local or otherwise – involved. We’re not against Google ads at all – we’ve worked with them a lot in the past. But not for roofing companies. Because they’re too haphazard, too competitive and far too expensive.
